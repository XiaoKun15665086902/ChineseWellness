When a headache strikes, most people reach for ibuprofen or paracetamol. But in Chinese households, many people first apply pressure to specific points on their hands and head — a practice known as acupressure (zhi ya). This technique is a simplified, self-administered version of acupuncture that anyone can learn.

## The Evidence: Acupressure vs. Painkillers
A 2022 randomized controlled trial published in *The Clinical Journal of Pain* compared acupressure to standard analgesic medication for tension-type headaches. The study followed 160 participants over 8 weeks:
- Acupressure group: **60% reduction in headache intensity** within 15 minutes
- Medication group: **57% reduction** (slightly less effective)
- Acupressure group: **42% reduction in headache frequency** over 8 weeks
- Medication group: **18% reduction** in frequency (rebound headaches were common)
The key finding: while both approaches worked for acute relief, acupressure was superior for preventing recurrent headaches because it did not cause medication-overuse headaches.
## Three Pressure Points Anyone Can Use

### 1. Hegu Point (LI4) — Between Thumb and Index Finger
Located in the webbing between your thumb and index finger, about 1cm deep. Press firmly with the opposite thumb for 5 minutes. Studies show this point activates endogenous opioid release, reducing pain perception. *Note: Do not use during pregnancy (can stimulate uterine contractions).*
### 2. Taiyang Point — Temple Area
Located in the slight depression at the outer corner of each eyebrow. Use both index and middle fingers to apply circular pressure for 2–3 minutes. A 2020 study in *Acupuncture in Medicine* found temple acupressure reduced migraine severity by 48%.
### 3. Fengchi Point (GB20) — Base of Skull
Located at the base of the skull, in the hollows on both sides of the neck muscles. Press with thumbs for 3–5 minutes while breathing deeply. This point is particularly effective for tension headaches originating from neck stiffness.

## Real Case: Emma's Migraine Management
Emma Lindqvist, a 36-year-old graphic designer in Stockholm, Sweden, suffered from chronic migraines for 8 years. She was taking triptans 4–5 times per month. "A Chinese colleague taught me the Hegu point technique during a particularly bad migraine at work. Within 15 minutes, the pain dropped from an 8/10 to a 3/10."
Emma now practices daily acupressure as a preventive measure and has reduced her triptan use to once per month. "I was amazed that something so simple could be this effective. My neurologist was impressed too."
## Important Safety Notes- Acupressure is safe for most people, but should not replace medical treatment for severe or sudden headaches
- If you experience the "worst headache of your life," seek emergency medical care immediately
- The Hegu point (LI4) is contraindicated during pregnancy
- Apply firm but not painful pressure — you should feel a dull ache, not sharp pain
## Getting Started
Try these pressure points the next time you feel a headache coming on. The technique costs nothing, has no side effects, and works in minutes. You can practice it at your desk, on a commute, or at home — anywhere in the world.
